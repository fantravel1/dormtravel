🎞 As Seen In

Movies & Culture That Made Cancun Legendary

Cancun didn't become the ultimate spring break destination by accident. Decades of movies, TV shows, and pop culture cemented its reputation as THE place to be when classes let out. Here's what put it on the map.

🎬

Spring Breakers

2012

Harmony Korine's neon-soaked fever dream starring Selena Gomez, Vanessa Hudgens, and James Franco became the defining visual of modern spring break culture. The film's sun-drenched party scenes and "Spring break forever" mantra turned Cancun's beach party aesthetic into an entire cultural moment that still dominates Instagram every March.

📺

The Real Cancun

2003

The first-ever reality movie dropped 16 strangers into an all-inclusive Cancun resort and filmed every chaotic minute. Before TikTok, before Instagram, this film showed America exactly what spring break in Cancun looked like -- and millions of college students immediately started planning their own trips. It basically invented the spring break content genre.

📻

MTV Spring Break

1986-2005

For nearly two decades, MTV broadcast live from Cancun's beaches, turning the Hotel Zone into the most famous party strip on the planet. Carson Daly, Jenny McCarthy, and a revolving cast of hosts brought concerts, contests, and absolute chaos to millions of living rooms. MTV single-handedly made Cancun synonymous with college spring break in America.

💎

Fool's Gold

2008

Matthew McConaughey and Kate Hudson's treasure-hunting adventure showcased the stunning Caribbean waters off Mexico's coast. While technically set in the Florida Keys and Bahamas, many scenes highlighted the exact turquoise waters and white sand beaches that make the Yucatan Peninsula irresistible. It made everyone want to grab a snorkel and jump into crystal-clear Mexican waters.

🏈

Blue Crush 2

2011

This surf-culture sequel followed a young woman chasing waves across stunning Caribbean coastlines. The film's gorgeous underwater cinematography and beach lifestyle scenes made the Yucatan's coastline look absolutely unreal. It inspired a generation of adventure-seeking college women to add water sports and beach exploration to their Cancun itineraries beyond just the pool parties.

🌴 Why Cancun

Why Cancun Is THE Spring Break Destination

Over 100,000 college students descend on Cancun every spring break season. Here's exactly why it dominates every other destination and why your squad needs to be there.

🍸

All-Inclusive Culture

Cancun practically invented the all-inclusive spring break. For one flat price you get unlimited food, unlimited drinks (yes, alcoholic), pool access, beach chairs, entertainment, and often nightclub deals. No mental math. No splitting checks. No budgeting stress. Just wristband on, brain off, and enjoy everything without reaching for your wallet. Resorts like Oasis Cancun Lite, Grand Oasis, and Temptation Resort are built specifically for the college crowd with foam parties, DJ pools, and organized events all day long.

✈

Direct Flights from Everywhere

Cancun International Airport (CUN) has direct flights from virtually every major US city. You can fly from New York, LA, Chicago, Dallas, Miami, Atlanta, Denver, and dozens more with flight times ranging from just 2 to 5 hours. Airlines like Spirit, Frontier, JetBlue, Southwest, and American all compete for spring break travelers, which means deals as low as $150-$300 round trip if you book early. It's literally one of the cheapest international flights you can take from the US.

🍺

Drinking Age is 18

Let's be honest -- this is a huge draw. Mexico's legal drinking age is 18, which means every single college student can legally enjoy the open bar at their all-inclusive, hit the clubs in the Party Center, and order margaritas on the beach without fake IDs or sketchy situations. Freshmen, sophomores, juniors, seniors -- everyone's in. Just bring your passport or a valid photo ID and you're golden. This alone makes Cancun more accessible than any US spring break destination.

🌊

World-Class Beaches

Cancun's beaches aren't just nice -- they're consistently ranked among the best in the entire world. We're talking powdery white sand that squeaks under your feet and water so turquoise it looks photoshopped. The Hotel Zone sits on a narrow strip between the Caribbean Sea and the Nichupte Lagoon, meaning you're surrounded by water on both sides. Playa Delfines, Playa Forum, Playa Tortugas, and Playa Chac Mool are all stunners. The beach content alone will carry your Instagram for the next six months.

🌈

Legendary Nightlife

Cancun's Party Center (also called the Hotel Zone nightlife strip) is one of the most concentrated nightlife districts in the Western Hemisphere. Coco Bongo is a multi-level show club with acrobats, confetti cannons, and celebrity impersonators. The City holds 5,000+ people and hosts world-class DJs. Mandala, La Vaquita, Palazzo, and Señor Frog's keep the energy going every single night. Most clubs include open bar with your cover charge ($40-$80). The party doesn't even start until midnight and goes until sunrise.

🛡

Tourist-Friendly & Safe

Cancun's entire Hotel Zone was purpose-built for international tourism. The 14-mile strip is heavily patrolled by dedicated tourism police, resorts have 24/7 security, and the local economy depends on keeping visitors safe and happy. You'll find English spoken everywhere, US dollars accepted at most places, and ATMs on every corner. Over 6 million tourists visit Cancun annually and the infrastructure is world-class. Stick to the Hotel Zone and tourist areas and you'll feel as safe as any major US city.

💰 Budget vs Baller

The Cancun Experience: Budget vs Baller

There's no wrong way to do Cancun spring break. Whether you're scraping together every dollar from your part-time job or your parents are feeling generous, both versions are unforgettable. Here's what each looks like in practice.

🃏 Budget Spring Break

$800 - $1,200 per person

Accommodation: Hostel dorm bed ($25-$35/night) or a budget hotel in the Hotel Zone like Oasis Cancun Lite. Split an Airbnb condo 4-6 ways and pay $30-$50 per person per night. You get a kitchen, a pool, and a balcony overlooking the lagoon.

Food: Street tacos from Tacos Rigo and the taco stands on Avenida Tulum for $1-$3 each. Fresh ceviche cups from beach vendors for $5. Convenience store snacks from Oxxo. If you have a kitchen, hit Walmart or Chedraui for groceries and make breakfast in the room.

Transportation: The R-1 public bus runs the entire Hotel Zone for 12 pesos ($0.70). It's safe, reliable, and runs until midnight. Uber rides are $3-$8 within the Hotel Zone. Skip the $50 airport taxis and book a shared shuttle for $15.

Nightlife: Hit happy hour specials (2-for-1 drinks at most Hotel Zone bars from 5-8 PM). Senior Frog's has free entry some nights. La Vaquita offers $30 open bar deals if you buy online in advance. Beach parties and hostel bar crawls are free or nearly free.

Activities: Public beaches are free. Ferry to Isla Mujeres is $15 round trip. Cenotes are $15-$30 entry. Tulum ruins are $5. You don't need to spend big to see the best of the Yucatan.

The Vibe: Scrappy, social, adventurous. You'll meet people from 20 different countries at the hostel. You'll eat the best tacos of your life for $2. You'll have stories that start with "So we were on this random public bus and..."

💎 Baller Spring Break

$1,800 - $3,000 per person

Accommodation: All-inclusive resort like Grand Oasis Cancun, Hyatt Ziva, or Live Aqua. Wristband on, wallet stays in the safe. Unlimited top-shelf drinks, gourmet restaurants, ocean-view rooms, and infinity pools. Some resorts have swim-up bars and rooftop jacuzzis.

Food: Buffet breakfast with omelette stations and fresh tropical fruit. A la carte Italian, Japanese, Mexican, and steakhouse restaurants included with your stay. Room service at 3 AM after the clubs. Never think about a food budget because it's all covered.

Transportation: Private SUV airport transfer waiting with a sign with your name on it. Resort shuttle to the Party Center. Uber Black for nightlife. Everything is seamless and air-conditioned.

Nightlife: VIP table at Coco Bongo with bottle service ($200-$400 split among the group). Skip every line. Dedicated VIP section with leather couches and a personal server. VIP entry to The City and Mandala included with premium packages.

Activities: Private catamaran to Isla Mujeres with your crew only. Parasailing over the Hotel Zone ($60). Jet ski rentals ($80/hour). Xcaret Plus package with private lockers, buffet, and premium snorkeling gear. Private cenote tours with a photographer.

The Vibe: Effortless, luxurious, Instagram-perfect. You'll never wait in line, never do mental math, and never wonder where your next meal is coming from. Everything is dialed in and premium.

💡 Pro Tip

Here's a secret the travel industry won't tell you: budget spring breakers almost always come home with BETTER stories. When you're navigating public buses, bargaining at taco stands, meeting strangers at hostels, and figuring things out on the fly, you're creating the kind of chaotic, hilarious memories that you'll still be laughing about at your 10-year reunion. The baller version is amazing and comfortable, but the budget version builds character and friendships. Either way, you're in Cancun -- you literally can't lose.

🏘 Accommodation

Where to Stay: Accommodation Deep Dive

Cancun has options for every budget and every vibe. From $25/night hostel dorms to $250/night luxury all-inclusives, here are the six best accommodation strategies for college spring breakers in 2026.

🎆 Grand Oasis Cancun

From $120/night all-inclusive

The Vibe: This is THE college party resort in Cancun. Grand Oasis is purpose-built for spring breakers with 3 massive pools, daily foam parties, live DJs from noon to midnight, bikini contests, and an on-site nightclub. The all-inclusive wristband covers unlimited food at 12 restaurants, unlimited drinks at 15 bars, and access to all pool and beach events. It's essentially a self-contained spring break city.

Best For: Groups who want the classic all-inclusive spring break experience with zero planning. Everything is included and the energy is non-stop from check-in to checkout.

Pros: All-inclusive eliminates budgeting stress. Massive property with tons of options. Right in the Hotel Zone. Spring break programming is top-tier. On-site nightclub means no taxi costs some nights.

Cons: Can feel overwhelming and crowded during peak weeks. Room quality varies -- request a renovated room. Food is quantity over quality. You're in a spring break bubble and won't experience "real" Cancun.

🌎 Hostel Mundo Joven Cancun

From $25/night (dorm bed)

The Vibe: The backpacker's base camp. Mundo Joven is a social hostel in downtown Cancun with mixed and female-only dorm rooms, a rooftop bar, pool table, communal kitchen, and organized bar crawl meetups every night during spring break. You'll share a room with 4-8 travelers from around the world and make instant friends over cheap beers on the rooftop. The hostel organizes group trips to cenotes, Isla Mujeres, and Chichen Itza at discounted rates.

Best For: Solo travelers, social butterflies, and anyone who values meeting new people over resort luxury. Also great for budget travelers who'd rather spend money on experiences than a hotel room.

Pros: Incredibly cheap. Built-in social scene. Organized activities and bar crawls. Kitchen saves money on food. Authentic downtown location near amazing local restaurants.

Cons: Downtown location means a 20-minute bus ride to the Hotel Zone beaches and clubs. Dorm rooms mean less privacy and potential noise. No beach access on-site. Need to take the R-1 bus or Uber to nightlife.

💻 Selina Cancun Downtown

From $60/night (private room)

The Vibe: Where digital nomad culture meets spring break energy. Selina is a trendy boutique hostel/hotel hybrid with co-working spaces, a pool, yoga classes, a bar, and curated social events. The rooms range from dorm beds ($30/night) to private rooms ($60-$90/night) with modern, Instagram-worthy design. The crowd skews slightly older and more international -- gap year travelers, remote workers, and adventurous college students who want more than just pool parties.

Best For: Students who want a social atmosphere with a design-forward aesthetic. Great for small groups of 2-3 who want private rooms without resort prices. Perfect if you're combining spring break with remote coursework or freelance projects.

Pros: Stylish and clean. Great pool and bar scene. Co-working space if you need to cram for midterms (it happens). Organized day trips and social events. More curated crowd.

Cons: Downtown location, not beachfront. Smaller property means it can sell out fast. Not a rager atmosphere -- if you want non-stop party energy, Grand Oasis is a better fit. Restaurant options on-site are pricier than street food.

🏠 Airbnb in Hotel Zone

$30-$50/person/night (split 4-6 ways)

The Vibe: The move for large groups. Rent a 2-3 bedroom condo in the Hotel Zone and split it 4-6 ways. You get a full kitchen (breakfast savings are huge), a living room for pregaming, a balcony with lagoon or ocean views, and often access to the building's pool and gym. You're right in the Hotel Zone within walking distance of beaches, clubs, and restaurants, but you have your own private space to retreat to.

Best For: Groups of 4-6 friends who want Hotel Zone location at budget prices. Ideal for groups with mixed budgets -- the frugal friend and the big spender can both be happy when the base cost is this low.

Pros: Best value per person in the Hotel Zone. Kitchen saves $20-$40/day on food. Private space for the group. No resort crowds. Walking distance to everything. More authentic living experience.

Cons: No all-inclusive perks -- you're buying all food and drinks separately. No organized activities or pool parties. Need to self-organize everything. Quality varies wildly -- read reviews carefully and book Superhosts only. Cleaning fees can add up.

✈ All-Inclusive Packages

From $799 with flights included

The Vibe: The "I don't want to think about anything" option. StudentUniverse, CheapCaribbean, and Apple Vacations offer bundled packages that include round-trip flights from major US hubs (JFK, LAX, ORD, DFW, MIA, ATL), 5-6 nights at an all-inclusive resort, airport transfers, and sometimes excursion credits. You pay one price, get a confirmation email, and show up at the airport. That's it. Everything else is handled.

Best For: First-time international travelers, groups who want zero logistical stress, and anyone who wants to lock in a fixed budget with no surprises. Also great for students who don't have time to research flights, hotels, and transfers separately.

Pros: One price covers everything. No booking 5 different things on 5 different sites. Often cheaper than booking flight + hotel separately. Student-specific platforms offer age-verified deals. Group rates available for 6+ people.

Cons: Less flexibility on dates and hotel choice. You get what's in the package -- upgrades cost extra. Peak spring break weeks sell out fast. Cheapest packages may fly out of inconvenient airports or at odd hours. Read the fine print on what's actually "included."

🏙 Downtown Cancun Hotels

From $40/night

The Vibe: The off-the-beaten-path play. Downtown Cancun (El Centro) is where locals live, eat, and hang out. Hotels here are significantly cheaper than the Hotel Zone, and you're surrounded by authentic Mexican restaurants, panaderias, street food stalls, and Parque de las Palapas -- a lively public square with live music and food vendors every evening. It's the "real Mexico" experience that most spring breakers completely miss.

Best For: Budget travelers, culture seekers, and students who want to experience Mexico beyond the resort bubble. Great for foodies -- the best and cheapest Mexican food in Cancun is downtown, not in the Hotel Zone.

Pros: 50-70% cheaper than Hotel Zone hotels. Incredible authentic food within walking distance (tacos al pastor for $1, tortas for $3, fresh jugos for $2). Feels like real Mexico. R-1 bus to Hotel Zone beaches takes 20 minutes and costs $0.70.

Cons: 20-30 minutes from Hotel Zone beaches and nightlife by bus or Uber. No beach access on-site. Less English spoken (bring Google Translate). Uber rides to nightlife add $5-$10 each way. You'll need to be more independent and adventurous.

💡 Pro Tip

The smartest move for groups of 4-6: book an Airbnb in the Hotel Zone for your base, then buy a 1-day pass to Grand Oasis or another all-inclusive resort ($60-$80 for a day pass). You get the best of both worlds -- cheap accommodations with a kitchen AND one epic all-inclusive pool party day. Some resorts sell "spring break day passes" that include open bar, food, pool, and beach access. Call ahead or check their website for day pass availability.

📅 Itinerary

Your 7-Day Cancun Spring Break Itinerary

The perfect balance of beach days, adventure excursions, nightlife, and recovery time. Follow this day-by-day guide and you'll hit every highlight without burning out by Day 3.

Day 1

Arrival & Hotel Zone Orientation

Touch down at Cancun International Airport (CUN), grab your shuttle or pre-booked transfer to the Hotel Zone, and get settled into your all-inclusive resort. Today is about getting your bearings, checking in, and easing into vacation mode.

Morning Arrive at CUN airport, clear customs (bring your passport and tourist card), and take your airport shuttle to the Hotel Zone. Pro tip: book your airport transfer in advance through your resort or a service like Canada Transfers for $15-$25 per person -- way cheaper than taxis ($50+).
Afternoon Check into your all-inclusive, get your wristband, claim your pool chairs, and take your first dip in the Caribbean. Hit the beach or pool bar for your first round of complimentary drinks. Walk the Hotel Zone strip to scope out restaurants and clubs for the week. Grab lunch at your resort's buffet or beachside grill.
Evening Ease into the nightlife with dinner at your resort's a la carte restaurant (included with all-inclusive), then head to Señor Frog's in the Party Center for an iconic first-night warm-up. It's casual, fun, and the perfect way to break the ice with your crew. Expect yard-long drinks, body shots, and dancing on tables. Cover is usually free or $20-$30 with open bar.
Day 2

Beach Day & Snorkeling at Punta Nizuc

Your first full day in paradise. Dedicate it to the beach, the water, and soaking up that vitamin D. This is the day for your best beach photos and your first taste of Cancun's incredible underwater world.

Morning Wake up to the all-inclusive breakfast buffet (omelette station, fresh tropical fruit, chilaquiles -- you're in Mexico, try them). Then claim your beach chairs early and spend the morning swimming in the Caribbean. The water is warm, clear, and impossibly blue. Hit Playa Delfines for photos with the iconic CANCUN sign.
Afternoon Book a snorkeling trip to the MUSA Underwater Museum at Punta Nizuc ($35-$50 per person). You'll swim over 500+ submerged sculptures on the ocean floor -- it's one of the most unique snorkeling experiences on Earth. Alternatively, hit up the resort's pool party. Many all-inclusives run DJ pool parties from 2-6 PM during spring break with drink specials and contests.
Evening Tonight is Coco Bongo night. This is the club everyone talks about -- a multi-level entertainment venue with live acrobatic shows, confetti drops, celebrity impersonators performing Michael Jackson and Beyonce, and absolute sensory overload. Get the Gold Member package ($60-$80) which includes open bar all night. Arrive by 11 PM for the best experience. It goes until 4 AM.
Day 3

Xcaret Eco-Park Adventure

Take a break from the party scene and experience the natural beauty that makes the Yucatan Peninsula one of the most incredible places on Earth. Xcaret is a world-class eco-archaeological park about an hour south of Cancun.

Morning Catch the Xcaret shuttle from your hotel zone pickup point at 8:30 AM (included with ticket). Arrive at the park and start with the underground rivers -- you'll float through crystal-clear cenote water in ancient limestone caves wearing a life vest and snorkel gear. It's surreal and unlike anything you've ever experienced. The water is cool and refreshing.
Afternoon Explore the butterfly pavilion (one of the largest in the world), snorkel in the natural inlet with tropical fish, visit the jaguar island, and check out the Mayan ruins on-site. Grab lunch at one of Xcaret's included restaurants if you booked the Plus package ($130-$150). The park has a tequila tasting area too -- because you're in Mexico and it would be rude not to.
Evening Stay for Xcaret's incredible nighttime show "Xcaret Mexico Espectacular" -- a massive production with 300+ performers showcasing Mayan history, traditional dances, and the famous Juego de Pelota (Mayan ball game). It's genuinely one of the best live shows in the Western Hemisphere. Shuttle returns you to the hotel zone by 10:30 PM. Take a chill night or hit a low-key bar.
Day 4

Isla Mujeres Catamaran Party

One of the absolute highlights of any Cancun trip. Board a catamaran, sail across the Caribbean to the gorgeous Isla Mujeres, and experience the best boat party of your college career. This day alone is worth the entire trip.

Morning Book the Isla Mujeres catamaran party tour ($60-$90 per person, includes open bar and lunch). Depart from Playa Tortugas or your tour pickup point around 9 AM. The catamaran ride itself is a party -- DJ, dancing, open bar starts immediately, and the views of the Cancun skyline from the water are absolutely fire. You'll stop for snorkeling at the El Farito reef where you can swim with tropical fish and sea turtles.
Afternoon Arrive at Isla Mujeres and explore this laid-back island paradise. Rent a golf cart ($35-$50 for the group) and cruise the island. Hit Playa Norte (consistently rated one of the best beaches in the entire Caribbean), grab fresh ceviche at a beachfront restaurant, visit the southern point with the Ixchel temple ruins, and browse the colorful shops on Hidalgo Avenue. The vibe is totally different from the Hotel Zone -- chill, authentic, and gorgeous.
Evening Sail back to Cancun on the catamaran with the sunset behind you (incredible photo op). You'll be sun-tired and happy. Grab dinner at one of the Hotel Zone's restaurants -- try Puerto Madero for upscale seafood or Thai Lounge for something different. If you still have energy, hit up Mandala or Palazzo for a more upscale club experience compared to the Coco Bongo chaos.
Day 5

Chichen Itza & Cenote Excursion

Today you're seeing one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Yes, really. Chichen Itza is a 2.5-hour drive from Cancun but absolutely worth the early wake-up call. You'll also swim in an ancient cenote -- a natural sinkhole sacred to the Maya.

Morning Your tour bus picks you up from your hotel at 7 AM (book through Viator or your resort concierge for $60-$100 per person including lunch and cenote entry). Arrive at Chichen Itza early to beat the crowds and the heat. Stand before the massive El Castillo pyramid (Kukulkan) and listen to your guide explain how the ancient Maya built this astronomical marvel over 1,000 years ago. Clap in front of the pyramid and hear the echo mimic the call of the quetzal bird. Mind = blown.
Afternoon After exploring the ruins, stop at Cenote Ik Kil (or Cenote Suytun depending on your tour). Descend into a massive open-air cave and swim in crystal-clear turquoise water surrounded by hanging vines and roots. It looks like something out of a fantasy movie and the photos are absolutely unreal. Enjoy a buffet lunch of traditional Yucatecan food -- cochinita pibil (slow-roasted pork), handmade tortillas, and agua de horchata.
Evening Return to Cancun around 6-7 PM. You'll be tired from the day but it's worth rallying for one more night out. Tonight try La Vaquita -- it's a loud, rowdy, hilarious party bar with foam parties, body paint, and DJs spinning reggaeton and EDM. The cover is around $40-$50 with open bar and the vibe is pure chaos in the best possible way. Or honestly, hit the resort hot tub and call it a night -- no judgment.
Day 6

Ultimate Pool Party & Farewell Night

Your last full day in paradise. No excursions, no alarms, no plans except maximum enjoyment of your all-inclusive resort and one final legendary night out. This is the day to squeeze every last drop out of Cancun.

Morning Sleep in. You've earned it after six days of non-stop action. Roll into the breakfast buffet at 10 AM, load up on huevos rancheros and fresh-squeezed orange juice, and park yourself by the pool. Many resorts run their biggest pool parties of the week on Saturdays -- foam machines, DJs, drink specials, limbo contests, and belly flop competitions. Enter everything. Win nothing. Have the time of your life.
Afternoon Hit the beach one final time. Playa Forum is the spring break epicenter -- it's right in front of the Party Center and the energy is electric during peak season. Take your final sunset photos, do some last-minute souvenir shopping at the Forum by the Sea mall (tequila bottles, Mexican blankets, funny spring break t-shirts), and grab your last beachside margarita. Hit the resort spa if your all-inclusive includes it -- a massage after a week of partying is chef's kiss.
Evening Go out with a bang. Tonight is The City -- one of the largest nightclubs in all of Latin America with a capacity of 5,000+ people. Multiple levels, world-class DJs, insane light shows, and an energy level that's hard to describe. Spring break nights here are absolutely electric. Cover is $50-$70 with open bar. Make friends with strangers, dance until 4 AM, and soak in every last second of your Cancun spring break. This is the night you'll talk about for years.
Day 7

Departure & Already Planning Next Year

All good things must come to an end. But the memories, the photos, the group chat stories, and the tan lines last forever. Time to head home and immediately start a savings fund for next year.

Morning Check out of your resort (most checkouts are by 12 PM but confirm with your hotel). Hit the breakfast buffet one last time and grab some final pool photos. Pack your bags and double-check you have your passport, phone charger, and all the souvenirs you impulse-bought at 2 AM.
Afternoon Take your airport transfer back to CUN. Arrive at least 3 hours before your international flight for customs and security. The Cancun airport has decent food options and a Duty Free shop where you can grab last-minute tequila, vanilla extract (a Mexico specialty), and hot sauce for your dorm room. Clear US customs and board your flight home.
Evening Land back in the US, open your camera roll, and realize you somehow took 2,000+ photos in 7 days. Start the group chat thread titled "Cancun 2027???" and begin the cycle all over again. Unpack, rehydrate, and start working on that post-spring-break glow-up for your Instagram dump.
💡 Pro Tip

Book your Cancun spring break package in November or early December for the best deals -- prices can be 20-30% cheaper than booking in January or February. Also, the second and third weeks of March are peak spring break weeks with the biggest crowds and best party atmosphere. If you want a slightly cheaper, less chaotic experience, go the last week of February or first week of April. Group bookings of 6+ people often unlock additional discounts at all-inclusive resorts.

🏝 Day Trips

Day Trip Adventures from Cancun

Cancun is more than just beaches and clubs. The Yucatan Peninsula is packed with ancient ruins, underground swimming holes, island paradises, and eco-parks that'll blow your mind. These six day trips are all easily doable from your Hotel Zone base and worth every penny.

🏛

Chichén Itzá

Cost: $90 per person (guided tour with transport, lunch, and cenote entry) • Travel Time: 2.5 hours each way by bus

One of the New Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The massive El Castillo pyramid (Temple of Kukulkan) is an astronomical marvel built over 1,000 years ago by the ancient Maya. Clap your hands at the base and the echo mimics the call of the sacred quetzal bird. Your tour includes a stop at Cenote Ik Kil for a swim in a jaw-dropping underground sinkhole surrounded by hanging vines. Yes, it's a long day. Yes, it's absolutely worth the early wake-up. You're standing in front of something that was built before Europe had universities -- let that sink in. Book through Viator or your resort concierge and opt for the early departure to beat the crowds and midday heat.

🏝

Isla Mujeres

Cost: $15 round-trip ferry + $35-$50 golf cart rental for the group • Travel Time: 30 minutes by ferry from Playa Tortugas

A laid-back island paradise just off the coast of Cancun and the polar opposite of the Hotel Zone energy. Take the Ultramar ferry ($15 round trip, runs every 30 minutes), rent a golf cart, and cruise the entire island in a few hours. Playa Norte on the north end is consistently ranked one of the top 10 beaches in the entire Caribbean -- powder-white sand, crystal-clear shallow water, and beachfront bars serving $3 cervezas. Drive to the southern tip to see the Ixchel temple ruins perched on a cliff overlooking the ocean. Browse the colorful shops and restaurants on Hidalgo Avenue. Grab fresh ceviche for $8 at a beachfront palapa. This is the day trip that everyone says was their unexpected favorite.

💧

Cenote Swimming

Cost: $15-$30 entry per cenote • Travel Time: 30 minutes to 2 hours depending on cenote

Cenotes are natural sinkholes filled with crystal-clear turquoise freshwater -- the Yucatan has over 6,000 of them and they're absolute Instagram gold. The most popular for spring breakers are Gran Cenote (near Tulum, $15 entry, incredible for snorkeling and photos), Cenote Ik Kil (near Chichen Itza, $10 entry, a massive open-air cave with hanging vines), Cenote Suytun (the famous one with the light beam and platform, $15 entry), and Cenote Azul (near Playa del Carmen, $7 entry, open-air with cliff jumping). Many tour companies offer "cenote hopping" tours that hit 3-4 cenotes in one day for $60-$80 including transport and lunch. Bring reef-safe sunscreen -- it's required by law at cenotes. Waterproof phone case is mandatory for these photos.

🌳

Xcaret Park

Cost: $100-$130 entry (Plus package includes food and locker) • Travel Time: 1 hour south of Cancun (shuttle included with ticket)

A world-class eco-archaeological park that combines nature, adventure, and culture into one incredible day. Float through underground rivers in crystal-clear cenote water wearing a life vest and snorkel. Snorkel in a natural ocean inlet with tropical fish. Visit the butterfly pavilion (one of the largest in the world), walk through a jaguar habitat, explore on-site Mayan ruins, and taste tequila at the distillery. Stay for the evening "Xcaret Mexico Espectacular" show -- a massive production with 300+ performers showcasing Mayan history, traditional dances, and the ancient ball game. It's genuinely one of the best live shows in Mexico. The Xcaret shuttle picks you up from the Hotel Zone and drops you back. Book online in advance for 10-15% discounts.

🏖

Tulum Ruins

Cost: $5 entry to the ruins (90 pesos) + $40-$60 for a guided tour with transport • Travel Time: 1.5 hours south of Cancun

The only Mayan ruins built on a cliff overlooking the Caribbean Sea. The views are absolutely unreal -- ancient stone temples perched on a bluff with turquoise water crashing below. The site is compact and walkable in about 90 minutes. The main structure, El Castillo, is the most photographed ruin in the Yucatan. After exploring the ruins, walk down the cliff path to the beach below and swim in the shadow of a 700-year-old civilization. Combine this with a cenote stop and Playa del Carmen visit for a packed day trip. Arrive early (gates open at 8 AM) to beat the tour bus crowds that arrive around 10 AM. Bring cash for entry and water -- it gets hot up on those cliffs with zero shade.

🌎

Playa del Carmen

Cost: Free to visit (just transport) • Travel Time: 30-45 minutes south of Cancun by ADO bus ($5) or colectivo van ($3)

A vibrant beach town 30 minutes south of Cancun with a completely different energy. Fifth Avenue (Quinta Avenida) is a pedestrian-only shopping and dining street stretching 20+ blocks with boutique shops, restaurants, bars, and street performers. The beach is gorgeous and less crowded than Hotel Zone beaches. Nightlife on 12th Street rivals Cancun's Party Center but with a more local, less touristy feel. Clubs and bars here are generally cheaper -- $20-$30 covers with open bar instead of $50-$80 in Cancun. It's also the jumping-off point for the Cozumel ferry if you want to add a dive island to your trip. Great as a half-day trip combined with Tulum or cenotes, or as a full chill day away from the Hotel Zone madness.

💡 Pro Tip

Don't try to cram every excursion into your trip. Pick 2-3 day trips max and leave the rest for beach and pool days. The biggest mistake spring breakers make is over-scheduling and burning out by Day 4. A solid mix: one adventure day (Xcaret or cenotes), one culture day (Chichen Itza or Tulum), and one island day (Isla Mujeres). Book excursions through Viator or GetYourGuide for verified reviews and free cancellation -- not from the random guys selling tours on the beach.

🌈 Nightlife

The Ultimate Cancun Nightlife Guide

Cancun's Party Center in the Hotel Zone is one of the most concentrated nightlife strips on Earth. Every major club is within walking distance of each other. Here are the six venues you need to know, ranked by vibe, with real prices and insider tips that'll save you money and maximize your nights out.

🎟 Coco Bongo

$80 open bar • MUST DO • Show + Nightclub

The Experience: This isn't just a club -- it's a full-blown theatrical experience combined with the most intense nightclub you've ever been in. Acrobats fly over the crowd on wires performing Cirque du Soleil-level stunts. Confetti cannons blast every 20 minutes. Celebrity impersonators perform as Michael Jackson, Beyoncé, Spider-Man, and The Beatles while you dance below. Giant balloons drop from the ceiling. LED screens flash synchronized visuals. It's sensory overload in the best way possible.

Music: Everything -- Top 40, reggaeton, EDM, hip-hop, rock classics, Latin. The DJ switches genres constantly to match the show acts.

Insider Tips: Buy your tickets online at cocobongo.com before you go -- walk-up prices are $10-$20 more and the line without pre-purchased tickets can be 45+ minutes. The Gold Member package ($80) includes open bar all night and is the standard choice. The Front Row VIP ($120) gets you a reserved table near the stage. Arrive by 10:30 PM to get a good spot. The shows start at 11:30 PM. This is the one club everyone MUST do at least once. Schedule it for your second or third night so you're warmed up but not burned out.

🌈 Mandala

$50 entry with open bar • Multi-Level Megaclub

The Experience: A sleek, multi-level nightclub with a massive dance floor, world-class sound system, and a rooftop terrace where you can catch your breath and enjoy the Hotel Zone views between sets. Mandala is known for its foam parties (usually Wednesday and Saturday nights during spring break) where the entire dance floor fills with foam while you dance. The lighting and production are top-tier -- think Las Vegas meets Cancun. The crowd is a mix of college spring breakers, international tourists, and well-dressed locals.

Music: EDM, house, reggaeton, and hip-hop. DJs lean heavily into high-energy dance music with reggaeton drops that get the crowd going wild.

Insider Tips: Book the open bar package online for $50 instead of $60-$70 at the door. The rooftop terrace is the move when you need a breather -- great views and cooler temperature. Foam party nights are the most popular, so arrive early (11 PM) for those. Dress code is enforced -- no flip-flops, tank tops, or swim trunks. Mandala is directly across from Coco Bongo, so many people hit one venue early and hop to the other after midnight.

🎵 The City

$40-$60 entry • Biggest Club in Latin America

The Experience: With a capacity of 5,000 people, The City is one of the largest nightclubs in all of Latin America. Multiple levels, a massive main dance floor, VIP terrace areas, and a beach club extension make this venue feel absolutely enormous. During spring break, The City hosts themed nights, international DJ residencies, and special events that draw thousands of college students from across North America. The sound system is stadium-grade and the light shows are jaw-dropping -- lasers, pyrotechnics, LED walls, and CO2 cannons.

Music: Primarily EDM and hip-hop with dedicated room/area splits. The main room goes hard with electronic and dance music from internationally touring DJs. The upper levels tend toward hip-hop and reggaeton.

Insider Tips: This is the best venue for your big group night out because the capacity means you won't feel sardined even on the busiest nights. Open bar packages run $40-$60 depending on the night and how far in advance you buy. Check their Instagram (@thecitycancun) for spring break event lineups and special DJ announcements. VIP tables for groups of 6-8 can be surprisingly affordable when split ($50-$80 per person including bottles). Save The City for your last or second-to-last night for maximum send-off energy.

🍺 Señor Frog's

Free-$30 entry • Spring Break Institution

The Experience: If Cancun spring break had an official mascot, it would be the wild-eyed frog on this venue's logo. Señor Frog's is part restaurant, part bar, part absolute chaos machine. This is where you'll find yard-long drinks ($15-$20), body shot competitions on the bar, conga lines through the venue, waiters dancing on tables, and the general energy of a place that has zero interest in being classy. It starts as a dinner spot at 7 PM and transforms into a raging party by 10 PM. The vibes are unapologetically rowdy, cheesy, and incredibly fun.

Music: Party music wall-to-wall -- hip-hop, pop, reggaeton, party rock classics. The DJ takes requests and the MC keeps the energy at maximum with drinking games and crowd interactions.

Insider Tips: This is the perfect first-night venue. The energy is accessible, the drinks are strong, and the atmosphere is pure spring break nostalgia. Entry is often free or very cheap early in the evening -- show up for dinner around 8 PM and stay for the party. The yard-long drinks are shareable (and you'll want to share them). Buy the souvenir cup -- it's a classic spring break memento. Señor Frog's is best earlier in the evening (8 PM - midnight) before heading to a proper nightclub like Coco Bongo or The City for the late-night hours.

🤡 La Vaquita

$30 open bar deals • Hilarious & Casual

The Experience: La Vaquita (literally "The Little Cow") is the club that doesn't take itself seriously and is infinitely better for it. The venue hands out ridiculous oversized costumes, hats, and props as you enter -- think giant sombreros, inflatable guitars, foam cowboy hats, and neon wigs. Everyone looks absolutely absurd and that's the entire point. The dance floor is packed, the drinks are strong, and the energy is pure group fun. It's the most "let loose" venue in the Party Center and perfect for groups who want to laugh as much as they dance.

Music: Reggaeton, hip-hop, Latin pop, and EDM. Heavy on the reggaeton and crowd-favorite party songs. The DJs know exactly what spring breakers want to hear.

Insider Tips: Buy the open bar deal online for $30-$35 instead of $40-$50 at the door (check their website or the Cancun Nightlife promoter booths on the strip). La Vaquita is the best venue for large groups because the costume/prop gimmick is a natural icebreaker and makes for hilarious group photos. The venue is right in the Party Center between Mandala and Coco Bongo, making it easy to club-hop. Best nights are Thursday and Saturday during spring break. No dress code -- come as you are, they'll dress you up anyway.

🎸 Dady'O

$25 entry • Less Touristy • Rock + Electronic

The Experience: The OG of Cancun nightlife. Dady'O has been rocking the Hotel Zone since 1986 and has a rawer, more underground energy than the mega-clubs. Built inside a cave-like structure, the venue has an intimate, dark, sweaty atmosphere that feels more like a real nightclub and less like a tourist attraction. The crowd is a genuine mix of locals, international tourists, and college students, which gives it more authentic energy. Dady'O is where you go when you want to actually dance and lose yourself in the music instead of watching shows or wearing silly hats.

Music: Rock, electronic, EDM, indie dance, and Latin rock. The playlist is more eclectic and less formulaic than the bigger clubs. Some nights feature live DJs spinning deep house and techno.

Insider Tips: At $25 entry (sometimes with open bar during spring break promos), Dady'O is the most affordable club on the strip. It's directly across from Coco Bongo, so it's easy to do both in one night. The cave-like interior means it gets HOT -- dress light and hydrate. Wednesday nights during spring break are legendary with special events and discounted entry. This is the best pick for music lovers who find Coco Bongo too chaotic and La Vaquita too silly. If your group has that one friend who's "not really into clubs," bring them here -- the energy is different and they'll probably love it.

💡 Pro Tip

Don't go out every single night. Seriously. The biggest rookie mistake is going hard at the clubs 6 nights in a row and being completely destroyed by Day 4. The veteran move is 3-4 big nights out (Coco Bongo, The City, La Vaquita, Señor Frog's) with chill nights in between where you enjoy the resort bar, grab dinner out, or hit the hot tub early. Also, most clubs are cash-only at the door for walk-ups, so withdraw pesos during the day. And ALWAYS buy your tickets online in advance -- you'll save $10-$20 per club and skip the worst of the lines.

🛡 Safety

Safety Tips for Cancun Spring Break

Cancun is one of the safest international spring break destinations for college students, but smart travelers stay informed. Follow these tips and you'll have a worry-free trip.

🏙

Stay in the Hotel Zone

The Hotel Zone (Zona Hotelera) is a purpose-built 14-mile tourist strip with dedicated tourism police, 24/7 resort security, and a constant flow of other tourists. It's one of the safest areas in all of Mexico for visitors. Everything you need -- beaches, clubs, restaurants, shops -- is within this strip. There's really no reason to leave it unless you're on an organized tour. If you do venture into downtown Cancun, stick to well-lit, busy areas and go with a group.

🤝

Use the Buddy System

This is the golden rule for any spring break trip. Never go to clubs alone, always leave with the people you came with, and share your live location with your friend group via iMessage or WhatsApp. Before going out each night, designate one person as the "responsible friend" who keeps track of the group. If someone wants to leave early, make sure they have a safe ride back to the resort. Look out for each other -- that's what good friends do.

🌊

Respect the Ocean

Cancun's Caribbean side can have strong currents and rip tides, especially on the east-facing beaches. Always check the flag system before swimming: green (safe), yellow (caution), red (dangerous, don't swim). Never swim at night or after drinking. The lagoon side (west-facing) is calmer and better for inexperienced swimmers. If you get caught in a rip current, don't fight it -- swim parallel to shore until you're out of the current, then swim back in. Most beaches have lifeguards during the day.

☀

Sun Protection is Serious

Cancun is at 21 degrees latitude -- the sun is significantly stronger than what you're used to in the US. Apply SPF 50+ sunscreen every 2 hours and after swimming. Wear a hat and sunglasses during peak hours (11 AM - 3 PM). A bad sunburn on Day 1 can ruin your entire trip. Buy reef-safe sunscreen (required at many eco-parks and cenotes). Drink tons of water throughout the day -- dehydration combined with sun exposure and alcohol is the number one reason students end up at the resort clinic.

💳

Money & Valuables

Use your resort's in-room safe for your passport, extra cash, and electronics when you go out. Carry only what you need -- one card, some pesos, your phone, and your room key. Keep your phone in a front pocket or crossbody bag (no back pockets in crowded clubs). Use ATMs inside banks or your hotel lobby for the best exchange rates, not street-side ATMs. Alert your bank before traveling so your cards don't get frozen. Tip in pesos when possible -- 15-20% is standard in Mexico.

🚗

Transportation Safety

Only use official taxis (look for the taxi sign and license plate starting with "T"), Uber (yes, Uber works in Cancun), or your resort's taxi service. Never accept rides from unofficial vehicles. The R-1 public bus runs the entire length of the Hotel Zone for just 12 pesos ($0.70) and is perfectly safe during the day. For nightlife, Uber is your best bet -- it's cheap, tracked, and you can share your ride status with friends. Pre-book airport transfers through your resort to avoid the aggressive taxi touts outside the terminal.

🎒 Packing

The Essential Cancun Packing List

Pack smart, pack light, and don't forget the stuff that'll save your trip. These eight items are the difference between "best week ever" and "I spent $80 replacing my phone at a Cancun electronics store." Learn from other students' mistakes.

☀

Reef-Safe Sunscreen (SPF 50+)

This isn't optional. Mexican law requires reef-safe (biodegradable) sunscreen at cenotes, eco-parks like Xcaret and Xel-Ha, and many public beaches. Regular sunscreen with oxybenzone and octinoxate damages coral reefs and you will literally be turned away at park entrances if you have the wrong kind. Buy a reef-safe SPF 50+ before you leave (brands like Sun Bum, Raw Elements, or Thinksport). It costs $12-$15 at Target or Amazon. Buying it in Cancun will cost you double. Apply every 2 hours and after swimming. The Yucatan sun at 21 degrees latitude is no joke -- a bad burn on Day 1 ruins the entire trip.

📱

Waterproof Phone Case

A $10 investment that protects your $1,000 phone. You'll be in water constantly -- pools, ocean, cenotes, catamaran parties, foam parties, boat tours. A universal waterproof pouch (brands like JOTO or Mpow) lets you take underwater photos, use your phone on the beach without sand damage, and survive the inevitable drink spill at the club. You can buy these on Amazon for $8-$12 for a 2-pack. Do not convince yourself you'll "just be careful." You will drop your phone in water. It's not a question of if, it's when. The cenote photos alone are worth the investment -- underwater shots in crystal-clear turquoise water are pure gold.

🔋

Portable Charger (20,000mAh)

Your phone is your camera, your map, your Uber, your group chat, and your lifeline. Clubs, beach days, and excursions will drain your battery faster than you think, and there's nothing worse than being at 2% at 1 AM trying to Uber home from The City. Get a 20,000mAh portable charger (Anker is the gold standard, $25-$35 on Amazon) -- it'll give you 4-5 full charges and last the entire trip. Charge it overnight and throw it in your bag every day. Share it with friends and become the most popular person in the group. Also bring a 6-foot charging cable -- hotel outlets in Mexico are often in weird places far from the bed.

👗

Light Cover-Up

Cancun is a beach destination but you can't walk into restaurants, malls, or clubs wearing just a swimsuit. Many Hotel Zone restaurants have a casual dress code that requires at least a cover-up or shorts and a shirt. Pack a lightweight linen shirt, sundress, or sarong that you can throw over your swimwear for transitions from beach to lunch to shopping. For nightlife, most clubs enforce a dress code -- no flip-flops, no tank tops on guys, no swimwear. Pack one "going out" outfit that's comfortable in the heat: a breathable button-down or nice t-shirt for guys, a light dress or romper for women. You don't need to dress up -- just not look like you just came from the pool.

💵

Cash in Small Bills

Mexico runs on tips and many street food vendors, taxi drivers, and small shops are cash-only. Withdraw pesos from ATMs inside banks or your hotel lobby (best exchange rates, safest locations) and keep small bills on hand: 50-peso and 100-peso notes are ideal. Budget 200-300 pesos per day for tips, street food, and small purchases. Tip 15-20% at sit-down restaurants, 20-50 pesos for hotel housekeeping per day, 30-50 pesos for airport shuttle drivers, and 10-20 pesos for bag handlers. US dollars are accepted at many tourist spots but you'll get a worse exchange rate. Pro tip: avoid exchanging money at the airport -- the rates are terrible. ATMs give you the bank rate which is significantly better.

🥾

Comfortable Walking Sandals

Not flip-flops. Actual walking sandals with a strap and some support. You'll log 10,000-15,000 steps daily between beach walks, exploring Chichen Itza, wandering Isla Mujeres, walking the Hotel Zone strip, and standing in club lines. Cheap flip-flops will give you blisters and zero arch support. Invest in a pair of Chacos, Tevas, Birkenstocks, or even sport sandals from Nike or Adidas. They need to be comfortable for walking AND appropriate for getting wet. You'll wear these 80% of the trip. For nightlife, pack one pair of clean sneakers or casual shoes -- many clubs won't let you in with sandals. Your feet will thank you on Day 5 when your flip-flop friends are limping.

💧

Reusable Water Bottle

Tap water in Cancun is NOT safe to drink. This is non-negotiable -- do not drink the tap water, do not use it to brush your teeth, do not get ice from unknown sources (resort and restaurant ice is purified and safe). Bring a reusable water bottle and refill it at your hotel's purified water station -- most resorts and many Airbnbs have filtered water dispensers or 5-gallon garrafones available 24/7. Staying hydrated is absolutely critical when you're combining sun, alcohol, dancing, and physical activities. Dehydration is the number one reason spring breakers end up at the resort medical clinic. A 32oz Nalgene or HydroFlask will save you from buying $3 plastic water bottles all week and reduce plastic waste.

💊

Motion Sickness Meds

Even if you don't normally get motion sick, pack Dramamine or Bonine just in case. The catamaran to Isla Mujeres, the ferry ride, snorkeling boat trips, and the 2.5-hour bus ride to Chichen Itza can all trigger unexpected nausea, especially if you're tired, dehydrated, or recovering from the night before. Chewable Dramamine (less drowsy formula) is your best bet -- take it 30 minutes before boat trips. The Caribbean can get choppy, and there's nothing worse than being seasick on a party catamaran surrounded by 50 people having the time of their lives. This is a $5 purchase at any pharmacy that could save an entire day of your trip. Also useful: Pepto-Bismol tablets for the inevitable "I ate something adventurous" stomach situation.

💡 Pro Tip

Pack everything in a carry-on if possible. Lost luggage on a 5-7 day trip is devastating and international bag claims take forever. A standard carry-on and personal item can fit everything you need for Cancun -- you're wearing swimsuits and shorts most of the time anyway. Roll your clothes instead of folding to save space. Keep your passport, phone charger, one change of clothes, and sunscreen in your personal item (backpack) in case your carry-on gets gate-checked. And leave room in your bag for souvenirs -- you will buy tequila at Duty Free. Everyone does.

Cancun Spring Break FAQ

The best time for Cancun spring break is mid-February through late March. March is the peak month with the biggest crowds and best party atmosphere, especially the second and third weeks when most US colleges have their spring break. February is slightly cheaper and less crowded but still warm (80-85°F). Early April works too but you'll miss the peak spring break energy. Weather is consistently sunny and hot with very little rain during these months. Book flights and hotels by November-December for the best deals.

Yes, Cancun's Hotel Zone is very safe for tourists and is heavily patrolled by dedicated tourism police. The Hotel Zone is a 14-mile strip of resorts, restaurants, and clubs built specifically for international tourism, and Mexico's government invests heavily in keeping it secure because tourism is a massive part of the local economy. Stick to the Hotel Zone and well-known areas, use the buddy system at night, only take official taxis or Uber, keep valuables in your hotel safe, and don't accept drinks from strangers. Over 30,000 college students visit during spring break season every year and the infrastructure is built for it.

The legal drinking age in Cancun and all of Mexico is 18 years old. This is one of the main reasons Cancun is so popular with college students -- even freshmen and sophomores can legally drink at bars, clubs, and all-inclusive resorts. You'll need a valid photo ID to enter clubs and purchase alcohol. Your passport is the best ID to carry, but most establishments also accept a US driver's license. Carry a photocopy of your passport as backup and keep the original in your hotel safe.

For spring break, all-inclusive is almost always the better deal for college students. An all-inclusive includes unlimited food, drinks (alcoholic and non-alcoholic), pool and beach access, and often entertainment for one flat price of $80-$200/night per person. A regular hotel might be $50-$80/night but you'll easily spend $50-$100/day on food and drinks separately. All-inclusive also eliminates surprise charges and makes budgeting way easier. The best spring break beaches are Playa Forum (aka Forum Beach) -- the epicenter of spring break action located right next to Coco Bongo and the Forum by the Sea mall. Playa Delfines is the most beautiful public beach with the iconic Cancun sign, perfect for Instagram. Playa Tortugas is great for water sports and has a public ferry to Isla Mujeres. Playa Chac Mool is a popular mid-Hotel Zone beach with calmer waters ideal for swimming. Playa Langosta has the calmest water on the Hotel Zone and is great for relaxed beach days. Most all-inclusive resorts also have their own private beach sections with lounge chairs, umbrellas, and waitstaff bringing drinks right to you.

Cancun's nightlife during spring break is world-class and absolutely legendary. The Party Center in the Hotel Zone is where the action concentrates. Top clubs include Coco Bongo (multi-level show club with acrobats, confetti, and celebrity impersonators -- cover $60-$80 including open bar), The City (one of the largest clubs in Latin America, capacity 5,000+, top DJs), Mandala (upscale nightclub with great music and a rooftop), La Vaquita (rowdy party bar with foam parties and body paint), Palazzo (trendy nightclub), and Señor Frog's (casual party restaurant/bar, great for early nights). Most clubs don't get going until midnight and stay open until 5-6 AM. Cover charges of $40-$80 almost always include open bar. Many resorts also host pool parties and foam parties during the day.

Cancun has incredible day trip options that go beyond the beach and clubs. Top excursions: Xcaret eco-park ($100-$130, all-day nature park with underground rivers, snorkeling, wildlife, and an amazing nighttime show), Xel-Ha ($90-$110, all-inclusive water park and snorkeling paradise), Isla Mujeres catamaran party ($60-$90, boat party with open bar + gorgeous island to explore), Chichen Itza ($60-$100, one of the New Seven Wonders of the World with a cenote swim included), cenote tours ($30-$60, swim in stunning natural sinkholes), Tulum ruins ($40-$70, clifftop Mayan ruins overlooking the Caribbean Sea), and the MUSA Underwater Museum snorkeling ($35-$50, swim over 500+ submerged sculptures).
