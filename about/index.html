👋 About Us

We're DormTravel

The world's most fun travel resource built exclusively for college students. No parents. No mixed-age content. Just students.

🎯 Our Mission

Empowering Students to Travel Smarter, Safer & Louder

DormTravel exists because college travel is different. It's not about luxury resorts or retirement cruises. It's about cramming 6 friends into an Airbnb, finding $5 street food in a new city, and creating the stories you'll tell at every reunion for the rest of your life.

We're not a booking site. We're not trying to sell you anything. We're a knowledge and inspiration hub that helps you answer: Where should we go? How do we afford it? What should we know before we go?

Every destination guide, budget tip, and safety resource on DormTravel is written specifically for the college experience. Because your trip to Cancun with your roommates is nothing like your parents' anniversary vacation.

📝 Content Philosophy

How We Create Our Guides

Evidence-based research is the foundation of everything we publish. Every destination fact is verified against multiple sources before it appears on DormTravel. Prices are checked quarterly to ensure our budget breakdowns reflect real-world costs, not outdated estimates from two years ago. Safety ratings come from official government sources, local tourism boards, and on-the-ground reports. When we say a hostel costs $25 a night or a city is safe for solo travelers, we've done the homework so you don't have to.

Student-first editing shapes every word on this site. All content goes through a review process designed to ensure it speaks directly to college students, not families planning a Disney vacation or retirees booking a river cruise. Our editors ask one question above all others: would a 20-year-old with a part-time job and a packed class schedule find this useful? If the answer is no, we rewrite it. We strip out jargon, skip the fluff, and focus on the details that actually matter when you're planning a trip between midterms and finals.

Safety is not an afterthought at DormTravel; it's built into our content DNA. We use a tiered safety system across all destinations and experiences: green means go with standard precautions, yellow means exercise elevated awareness, and red means significant risks that require serious preparation or reconsideration. This system covers everything from general crime and natural hazards to nightlife safety, scam prevalence, and LGBTQ+ traveler considerations. We believe informed students make smarter decisions, and smarter decisions lead to better trips.

Our content is never "set it and forget it." Guides are refreshed seasonally to reflect current prices, changing conditions, new flight routes, and real student feedback. When a destination's vibe shifts, when a popular hostel closes, or when a new budget airline launches a route, we update our guides. Students who visited a destination can flag outdated information, and our editorial team reviews and incorporates feedback on a rolling basis. The travel landscape changes fast, and DormTravel changes with it.

❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is DormTravel free to use?

Yes, DormTravel is 100% free for all college students. Every destination guide, budget breakdown, safety resource, and trip idea on our site is available at no cost. We believe access to quality travel information shouldn't depend on your bank account. You'll never hit a paywall, be asked to subscribe, or need to create an account to read our content.

Does DormTravel book trips or is it just information?

DormTravel is an information and inspiration resource, not a booking platform. We provide the research, guides, budget breakdowns, and safety information you need to plan your trip. When you're ready to book, we point you toward reputable booking platforms, student discount programs, and deals. Think of us as the friend who's already been there and tells you everything you need to know before you go.

How does DormTravel make money?

DormTravel is supported through affiliate partnerships with travel companies, hostels, and student discount programs. When you click through to a booking platform from our site and make a purchase, we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. Importantly, affiliate relationships never influence our rankings, recommendations, or reviews. Our editorial content is completely independent from our revenue model.

Can I contribute to DormTravel or share my travel story?

Absolutely! DormTravel thrives on real student experiences. If you've recently traveled and want to share tips, recommendations, or your story, we'd love to hear from you. Student contributors help keep our content authentic, current, and diverse. Reach out through our contact page with a brief summary of your trip and what you'd like to share, and our editorial team will get back to you.
